Before you apply

These jobs were checked and look real. Read the posting, then apply.
It's a scam if a job ever asks you to:
  • Pay money or buy equipment to start
  • Cash a check and send part back, or buy gift cards
  • Give your Social Security or bank number before a real interview
  • Only talk by text, Telegram, or WhatsApp

Tips for applying

  • "Pay not listed" is normal — ask what it pays when you apply.
  • Applying in person? Bring your ID and a list of past jobs with dates.
  • A real employer will invite you to a phone call or an in-person interview — never just texting.
  • No answer after a week? It's okay to call and ask about your application.

Questions people ask

Are these jobs real?

Every job here came from the employer's own hiring system or a checked job site, and each one was screened for scams before you ever see it. Jobs that looked wrong were removed — the number we removed is shown at the bottom of the Jobs page.

What does “Will train” mean?

The employer wrote in their own posting that no experience is needed or that they provide training. Those are great ones to try even if you don’t feel qualified.

What if I don't meet everything they ask for?

Job ads are wish lists. If you can do about half of what they list, apply anyway — that's normal and employers expect it.

Why does it say “Pay not listed”?

The employer didn’t post the wage. That’s common and not a bad sign — ask what it pays when they contact you.

How does the work-search log work?

When you mark a job Applied, it's saved with the date automatically. The My apps tab can print or copy your weekly list for your Iowa unemployment claim.

Is my information private?

Everything stays on your phone unless you choose to sign in. Signing in saves your jobs, notes and chats to a private account so a new phone doesn’t lose them. It’s never sold, never shown to employers, and never used for ads. The only person who could ever see what’s saved is the person who set this up for you — nobody else.
